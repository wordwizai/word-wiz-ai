## Product 1: Lexia Core5 Reading

### Overview
- **Company:** Lexia Learning (Rosetta Stone subsidiary)
- **Target Audience:** K-5 students, schools
- **Focus:** Reading intervention and skill building
- **Approach:** Adaptive learning, data-driven instruction

### Pricing
- **School/District Pricing:** $50-80 per student per year (volume discounts available)
- **No free tier** (only trials for schools)
- **Licensing:** Annual subscription, per-student

### Core Features
1. **Adaptive Learning Technology**
   - Automatically adjusts difficulty based on performance
   - Individualized learning paths for each student
   - Pre-tests determine starting level

2. **Six Areas of Reading**
   - Phonological awareness
   - Phonics
   - Structural analysis
   - Automaticity/fluency
   - Vocabulary
   - Comprehension

3. **Progress Monitoring**
   - Detailed performance reports
   - LEXILE level tracking
   - Risk alerts for struggling students
   - Real-time data for teachers

4. **Curriculum Alignment**
   - Aligned to Common Core State Standards
   - Science of Reading based
   - Systematic and explicit instruction

5. **Offline Mode**
   - Limited offline capability
   - Primarily requires internet connection

### Strengths
- Comprehensive assessment system
- Research-backed pedagogy
- Excellent teacher dashboard and reporting
- Proven efficacy in schools (peer-reviewed studies)
- Identifies at-risk students early
- Systematic skill progression
- Used in 20,000+ schools nationwide

### Weaknesses
- Expensive ($50-80/student/year)
- No individual/parent purchase option
- No speech recognition or pronunciation feedback
- Can feel drill-based and repetitive
- Limited engagement for some students
- Requires school/district purchase
- Not designed for home use

### Best For
- Schools needing intervention data and reporting
- Title I programs
- RTI (Response to Intervention) frameworks
- Districts with budget for per-student licensing
- Teachers needing diagnostic assessments

### Technology
- Web-based platform
- iPad app available
- No speech recognition
- Algorithm-driven adaptation
- Tracks 100+ skills

## Product 2: Raz-Kids (Learning A-Z)

### Overview
- **Company:** Learning A-Z (Cambium Learning Group)
- **Target Audience:** K-5 students, classroom teachers
- **Focus:** Leveled reading practice and fluency
- **Approach:** Guided reading with comprehension quizzes

### Pricing
- **Classroom License:** $120-140 per year (up to 36 students)
- **School/District:** Volume pricing available
- **Individual:** Not available for direct parent purchase
- **Free Trial:** 14 days for teachers

### Core Features
1. **Leveled Reading Library**
   - 29 levels (aa - Z2)
   - 400+ eBooks and eQuizzes
   - New books added monthly
   - Printable book versions

2. **Reading Modes**
   - Listen: Hear book read aloud
   - Read: Independent reading
   - Quiz: Comprehension check
   - Recording: Student can record themselves

3. **Progress Tracking**
   - Reading level advancement
   - Comprehension quiz scores
   - Time spent reading
   - Books completed

4. **Gamification**
   - Star rewards for completing books
   - Robot avatar customization
   - Motivation through rewards
   - Kid-friendly interface

5. **Teacher Tools**
   - Assign specific books
   - Set reading level for each student
   - Monitor progress reports
   - Group students by level

### Strengths
- Massive library of leveled books (400+)
- Affordable for classrooms ($120/yr for 36 students = $3.33/student)
- Students love the gamification (robots, stars)
- Easy to use for teachers and students
- Works on most devices (iPad, Chromebook, PC)
- Great for building reading fluency and stamina
- Recording feature allows practice
- Printable books available

### Weaknesses
- No phonics instruction (assumes decoding skills)
- No pronunciation feedback on recordings
- Leveled readers (not decodable texts)
- Can encourage guessing from pictures/context
- Comprehension quizzes are basic multiple choice
- No adaptive technology (teacher assigns levels)
- Limited for struggling decoders
- Not Science of Reading aligned (uses leveled text system)

### Best For
- Classroom independent reading practice
- Students who can already decode
- Building reading fluency
- Homework/home practice
- English Language Learners (ELL) with basic decoding
- Schools wanting affordable reading practice

### Technology
- Web-based (Raz-Kids.com)
- iOS and Android apps
- Recording feature (no analysis, just playback)
- No speech recognition
- Simple interface

## Product 3: Word Wiz AI

### Overview
- **Company:** Word Wiz AI
- **Target Audience:** K-3 students (ages 4-9)
- **Focus:** Pronunciation feedback and phonics practice
- **Approach:** AI-powered speech recognition with phoneme-level analysis

### Pricing
- **Free Tier:** Core features free forever
  - Unlimited pronunciation practice
  - Speech recognition and feedback
  - Teacher dashboard (for educators)
- **Premium:** Optional upgrades (future)

### Core Features
1. **Phoneme-Level Speech Recognition**
   - wav2vec2-TIMIT-IPA model
   - Identifies specific pronunciation errors (e.g., "th" vs "f")
   - Real-time feedback
   - IPA phoneme analysis

2. **AI-Powered Personalization**
   - GPT-4 generates custom feedback
   - Adapts to individual pronunciation patterns
   - Encouraging, age-appropriate responses

3. **Practice Modes**
   - Unlimited Practice: Endless sentences
   - Story Mode: Sequential reading
   - Choice Story: Student-directed narrative

4. **Teacher Features**
   - Class management dashboard
   - Student progress tracking
   - Pronunciation error reports
   - Free for all teachers

5. **Technology**
   - Web-based (no download required)
   - Works in any modern browser
   - Optional client-side processing
   - WebSocket/SSE for real-time feedback

### Strengths
- **ONLY** free option with speech recognition
- Phoneme-level pronunciation feedback (unique)
- Teacher dashboard included free
- No per-student cost (unlimited students)
- Actually listens and corrects pronunciation
- Science of Reading aligned (phonics-based)
- Works on any device with browser
- Provides specific feedback (not just "try again")

### Weaknesses
- Not a full curriculum (narrow focus on pronunciation)
- No leveled book library (like Raz-Kids)
- No comprehensive assessment system (like Lexia)
- Limited progress tracking (compared to Lexia)
- Newer product (less established than competitors)
- Requires microphone access

### Best For
- Pronunciation practice and correction
- Students who can decode but mispronounce words
- Supplement to existing reading curriculum
- Budget-limited schools and teachers
- Home practice for parents
- English Language Learners (ELL) with pronunciation needs
- Complementary to Lexia or Raz-Kids

### Technology
- Cutting-edge: wav2vec2-TIMIT-IPA speech model
- GPT-4 for feedback generation
- Browser-based (HTML5 Web Audio API)
- Real-time processing (WebSocket)
- Client-side ML option (Transformers.js)
