- Definition: Ability to hear/manipulate individual sounds in words

- Examples: /c/ /a/ /t/ = cat

**Section 2: Developmental Progression**
- Ages 3-4: Rhyming awareness
- Ages 4-5: Syllable awareness, beginning sounds
- Ages 5-6: Blending, segmenting

- DIY assessment activities
- When to be concerned
- **Word Wiz AI can assess pronunciation of individual phonemes**

**Section 4: 15 Phoneme Awareness Activities (No Materials Needed)**
1. Rhyming games ("What rhymes with cat?")
2. Sound isolation ("What's first sound in 'sun'?")
3. Blending sounds (Parent: "/c/ /a/ /t/", Child: "cat!")
4. Segmenting words (say word, child breaks into sounds)
5. Sound deletion ("Say 'cat' without /c/")
6. Sound substitution ("Change /c/ in 'cat' to /b/")
7-15: Additional oral activities

**Section 5: Common Challenges & Solutions**
- Can't blend sounds → Start with 2-sound words
- Can't isolate sounds → Use exaggerated pronunciation

- Definition: Books leveled by difficulty (A-Z)
